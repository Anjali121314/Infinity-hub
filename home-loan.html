Home Loan Insurance

Home Loan Insurance, which is also known as Home Loan Protection Plan (HLPP), is a scheme offered by almost every financial institution in which the insurer will settle the borrower's outstanding or balance home loan amount with the lender or bank in the event of unforeseen circumstances, which may include the borrower's death.

Home Insurance Versus Home Loan Insurance

The following table represents the difference between home insurance and home loan insurance:

Categories Home Insurance Home Loan Insurance
Meaning It pays for the loss or damages if the house structure or its contents are damaged due to insured perils. It pays the remaining home loan amount of the insured if a mishap makes it impossible for them to pay off the loan.
Availability It can be purchased irrespective of regardless of whether you have a home loan. It can be purchased only if home insurance is available.
Down Payment No impact on the down payment. It reduces the down payment on your house.
Premium The premiums are lower than for home loan insurance. The premiums are higher as compared to home insurance.

Home Loan Insurance Benefits

  • Tax Benefit: Purchasing a home loan protection plan is an excellent method to obtain a tax break. Section 80C allows you to deduct the premium paid for insurance. 
  • Affordable: Property insurance for house loan policies uses a one-time premium method in which the premium is paid all at once. If a person's family cannot afford it, the premium can be added to the loan amount and deducted through EMI. This reduces the cost of paying the premium. 
  • Protection: Repaying a loan on behalf of someone else might be difficult. If you are unable to repay it, the collateral used to secure the loan may be seized. Home loan insurance will not allow you to get to that point. It ensures that a borrower's family does not have to go through a tough period following the death of a critical family member. 

Characteristics of Home Loan Protection Insurance

  • Payment in one lump sum: The premium for the home loan insurance cover can be paid all at once. Buyers can also combine the premium payment with the entire loan amount and pay it in monthly installments. 
  • Joint house loan borrowers: The home loan insurance plan covers all co-applicants who are on the same loan. Thus, if three family members insure their home loan, the untimely death of one of them will be protected by the home loan insurance plan. 
  • Riders and add-ons: The basic HLPP will cover the borrower's death. However, by adding a rider scheme that carries an additional premium, your home loan protection plan can cover severe conditions such as heart attack, cancer, and traumas. The add-ons can also cover disability and unemployment for up to 6 months of EMI payments.

Home Loan FAQs

What is a home loan?

A home loan is essentially a financing option where funds are provided to an individual or an entity for the purchase, construction, extension, or renovation of a residential or commercial property. Lenders provide funds upfront, and borrowers repay through monthly installments, usually over many years. It's crucial for prospective homeowners to understand the terms, interest rates, tenure, and eligibility criteria before applying.
