About Education Loan Insurance

Student loans, at the time of application, help you cover all your expenses associated with your education. However, the aftermath and financial burden of student loans might deter many and leave them worried about repaying the loan. Unemployment, demise, or financial difficulties may make loan repayment difficult.

This can prevent many students from opting for education loans in the first place. This is where education loan insurance proves to be most useful.

Borrowers and their families could potentially encounter significant financial challenges in its absence. Simply put, education loan insurance offers an essential safety net in the event that circumstances worsen and the loan applicant is unable to repay the loan balance. Today, we will explore the process, benefits and process of availing education loan insurance.

What is Education Loan Insurance?

Education loan insurance protects individuals and families against the financial burden of overdue student loans, especially in the event of failing to reimburse the loan. It prevents student loan debt from growing.

The financial risk associated with student loans is greatly reduced with education loan insurance. Education loan insurance covers the remaining student loan debt if the borrower dies, becomes disabled, or is involuntarily unemployed. This coverage eases the burden on the borrower's family or co-signers, who must repay the loan amount.

Is it Mandatory to Take Insurance for an Education Loan ?

It is not compulsory to opt for education loan insurance, as the requirement varies from one lender to another. For loans taken from government banks, it is not necessary to get insurance.

However, some private banks or organizations may require insurance as a condition for the loan. Most government banks allow the applicant to decide on the necessity of purchasing insurance for secured education loans.

Applicants applying for abroad education loans processed by private banks and NBFCs without collateral must buy education loan insurance. This is mandatory because the lender does not require the applicant to pledge collateral security against the loan.

What is Covered Under Education Loan Insurance?

Education loan insurance typically covers the undermentioned expenses:

Tuition Fees

Under education debt insurance, the school's outstanding tuition is covered. The insurance policy may cover tuition if the borrower cannot return the loan due to death, incapacity, or serious illness. This prevents the borrower's schooling from being interrupted and relieves the family of these costs.

Living Expenses

Education frequently requires housing, food, and other expenditures. Education loan insurance may cover these living expenditures, protecting the borrower and their family from unexpected financial hardship.

Loan Repayment Protection

Education debt repayment protection may be its most important covering. Insurance covers debt repayment in the case of incapacity, serious illness, or death. This relieves the borrower's family or co-applicant of the financial burden of repaying the loan, enabling them to concentrate on their well-being or grief process without added stress.

Pre-Existing Medical Conditions

Some student loan insurance programmes cover pre-existing medical issues, protecting borrowers against health discrimination. Note any pre-existing condition restrictions or limits while reviewing the insurance.

Benefits of Education Loan Insurance in India

Investing in education requires a substantial financial commitment. However, obtaining an education loan insurance policy can offer several advantages, protecting borrowers and their families from potential financial difficulties. Here are some potential benefits:

Financial Protection

Education loan insurance protects student loan debtors financially. It protects borrowers from defaulting on loans due to unanticipated situations.

Coverage

the comprehensive coverage that education loan insurance offers is among its primary advantages. These insurance usually cover the whole loan sum to protect the borrower, family, or co-applicant. Thus, they avoid the financial strain of repaying the amount.

Reduced Burden

Education loan insurance may help borrowers and co-applicants repay their debts. This allows students to concentrate on their studies and careers without worrying about money.

Support in Unforeseen Circumstances

Education loan insurance helps in unexpected situations like terminal sickness, permanent handicaps, and others. Due to the policy, borrowers and their families may rest confident that their financial responsibilities will be paid even in tough times.

Tax Benefits

Besides providing students with financial security and reassurance, education loan insurance also offers them tax advantages. As per the provisions outlined in sections 80C and 10(10D) of the Indian Income Tax Act, premiums paid for education loan insurance policies by debtors may qualify for tax deductions.

Interest Rate Concession

Certain education loan insurance schemes provide an additional benefit to debtors who choose to purchase insurance coverage, which is a reduction in the interest rate. Considering the substantial cost reductions that can be realized throughout the loan repayment period, this supplementary advantage further enhances the appeal of education loan insurance for frugal borrowers.

Education Loan FAQs

How are education loan insurance premiums calculated?

Premiums are typically calculated as a small percentage of the total outstanding loan amount and may vary based on factors such as loan tenure, borrower's age, and policy type.

Can education loan insurance premiums be included in the loan amount?

Yes, most lenders allow borrowers to include the insurance premium in the total loan amount, avoiding the need for a separate upfront payment.

What are the different types of education loan insurance policies?

Common types include loan repayment protection insurance, loan deferment protection insurance, and loan forgiveness insurance, each offering varying levels of coverage.

What happens to an education loan if the borrower dies in India?

If the borrower of an education loan dies in India, the repayment responsibility automatically falls on the co-signer or guarantor, who is typically a parent or close family member. The bank or financial institution will reach out to the co-signer/guarantor to continue the loan repayment process.

Is insurance mandatory for education loan?

Education loan insurance varies among lenders. Government banks don't mandate it, while NBFCs and private banks offering unsecured study-abroad loans require it. It protects students without collateral security.
